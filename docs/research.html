When, where and what cultivate: An optimization model for rural property planning

Sustainable production and land use planning are recurring challenges in the agricultural and forestry activities. In this context, a mathematical programming model is proposed that allows for both optimal production planning and its updating, without land exploitation. The proposed model, based on cash flows, maximizes the overall profit and includes two types of decision variables: the first relates to design of new activities, while the second concerns adaptation of existing activities.
